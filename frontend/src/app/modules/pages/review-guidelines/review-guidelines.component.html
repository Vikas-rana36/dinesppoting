General Guidelines

People use Dinespotting to connect with great local restaurant and beverage businesses. We understand that people won’t always agree, but we anticipate that everyone on the app/website treats one another and the platform with respect and truthfulness. We’ve gathered these general guidelines to help set the intonation for discourse on the app/site - Just in case.

Please go through the additional guidelines below for specific types of content that you may post on the app/site.

Relevance

Please ensure your contributions are relevant to the forum. For instance, reviews are not the place for raving about business employment practices, political ideologies, unfortunate circumstances, or other matters that don’t help customer experience.

Conflict of Interest

Your contributions to Dinespotting should be unbiased and impartial. For example, you shouldn’t write a review of your business or employer, your friend’s or relative’s business, your peers or competitors in your industry, or the business in your networking group. Also, businesses should not ask customers to write reviews.

Promotional Content

Don’t post promotional content unless related to Dinespotting advertising products or through a business account. Please, keep the app/site valuable for potential users and not overrun with commercial noise from every user.

Inappropriate Content

Images and colorful expressions are welcomed, but there’s no place for threats, lewdness, profound language, harassment, or other display of bigotry.

Privacy

Don’t promulgate people’s private information. For example, please don’t post photos or videos of other patrons without their permission, and don’t post other people’s full names unless you are referring to someone commonly referred to by their full name.

Intellectual Property

Don’t swap content from other sites, platforms, users, or businesses. So, write your own copy and share your own photos and videos.

Review Guidelines:

The best reviews are passionate and personal and portray your customer experience. They should offer a rich narrative, complete detail, and helpful tip for other customers. Here are some suggestions for conscientious reviews:

Incentivized Reviews

If you receive a free or discounted service from a business in exchange for posting a review:

  • You should clearly and conspicuously disclose the fact by including the expression at the start of the review, “I have this dish for free or at a discount for my review.” You may indicate that the review nonetheless reflects your honest opinion.
  • The business should not direct the content of your review, including by needing the review to be positive.

Dinespotting doesn’t permit reviews that are posted in exchange for compensation of any other kind.

Review updates:

Review updates should present a new experience or interaction with the business. Don’t keep posting about the same story you’ve already shared. If you’d like to add new insights to an old review, you can edit your review within (15 days) of posting it.

Review Process:

In some scenarios, Dinespotting may contact you regarding your review's content before it gets published. You agree to monitor your email after giving a review so that you can quickly respond to Dinespotting. You’ll be asked to agree to our Terms of Service and these Review Guidelines when you provide a review.

Inappropriate Reviews

You can contact us to report a review as inappropriate. We will then check if the review violates these guidelines or Dinespotting Terms of Use. Reviews are analyzed and evaluated from the perspective of what a potential customer would think.

Note that the Dinespotting app/site provides a forum for customers to share both positive and negative experiences and opinions. We don't arbitrate disputes and will not remove reviews because they are harmful and absent highly unusual circumstances. However, in our sole discretion, we reserve the right to determine, err on the transparency side, and comply with our policy to leave reviews published. To balance this "policy," businesses may submit responses to reviews.

Reviews may be removed for any of the following reasons:

  • Inappropriate content: Don't post reviews that contain or link to unlawful content, content that violates our user agreement, or infringes on privacy.
  • Conflict of interest: Reviews are only valuable when they are honest and unbiased. For example, don't post reviews about your business, a competitor, or a current/former employer.
  • Misrepresentation: Don't misrepresent your identity or affiliation with the company being reviewed, such as submitting a review on behalf of a client.
  • Others' experience: Don't submit reviews that do not reflect your own experience but those of another party.
  • Accuracy: Make sure your review is factually correct. Don't exaggerate or misrepresent your experience. We don't take sides regarding factual disputes, so we expect you to stand behind your review.
  • Off-topic reviews: Reviews are not a forum for personal rants or crusades, nor are they meant to be a forum for general political or social commentary. Stick to the facts about your experience.
  • Outdated reviews: Reviews should be from the latest experience varying up to (yyy years)

Photo and Video Guidelines

Photos and videos should be relevant to the business and present the typical consumer experience. If you’re a business, present what you offer and what is your Unique Selling Point that makes your business unique. Here are some additional things to keep in mind:

  • Keep it relevant to everyone: We may remove a photo or video that showcases a uniquely personal experience that is not related to other people (e.g., a receipt or email exchange with a business) from the photo gallery for the business, but it may appear alongside your review if you write one about the business.
  • Keep it clean: We don’t want imagery of violence, drug use, nudity, near nudity, or suggestive acts—even if those images are related to the business’s services or products.
  • Keep it friendly: Don’t use your photos or videos to attack or disparage others. An image is worth 1,000 words, so it’s ideal to choose those words precisely and keep your point clearly in a review, direct message, or review response.

Community Guidelines

Use your account to let people know who you are and what makes you tick. Your peers want to read reviews from people they know and rely on.

User Profile: Since user trust and transparency are essential to us, anonymous users who are disruptive to the community will be dealt with more sternly than those who stand behind their words. We encourage users to tell people a little something about themselves but ask that you keep it relevant and don’t attack competitors, reviewers, or Yelp. Avoid seeding your profile with keywords, special offers, or promotions—we’ll remove those if we see them.

Consumer Messaging: Dinespotting has an easy-to-use messaging feature to keep you connected with your fellow peers. However, kindly don’t use this feature to harass fellow users or send inappropriate content, and remember, customer accounts are for personal, non-commercial use only.

Business Messaging: Businesses should message reviewers using their free Business Account. Direct messaging is usually considered the best way to address a dispute with an unsatisfied customer. Still, you can also publicly resolve issues by posting a comment in response to a specific review. As with customers, we ask that business representatives upload a clear photo of themselves to help personalize their messaging. Remember, don’t use direct messages or public comments for personal attacks or offer an incentive to change a review.

Ask The Community: Being a part of a community where you can ask and answer questions about a business is exceptionally valuable, but as the cliche is, with great power comes great responsibility. Adhere to questions that are also relevant to others, don’t ask loaded questions or make presumptions, and keep the tone non-argumentative and civil. Suppose you don’t have anything meaningful to add; better not to say anything. Clear questions with enough context and details will get more focused answers and meaningful conversations.
